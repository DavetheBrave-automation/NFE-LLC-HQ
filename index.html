TOKEN MANAGEMENT RULES
Reference: SpitzLift Chrome session hit 206,408 tokens and timed out
1

HARD LIMIT per Chrome session: 20 tool calls. Write this at the top of every Chrome prompt. Without a written limit, Chrome will go until it runs out of tokens or the session dies.

2

SUMMARIZE AS YOU GO. Never ask Chrome to "read the full page" — always "extract and summarize only." Full-page reproductions are the single biggest token killer.

3

SPLIT BY TOPIC not by time. Each Chrome session owns one topic completely. Don't mix leadership research with competitive research in the same session.

4

REPORT FORMAT FIRST. Start every Chrome session with "Keep total output under [X] words" — this forces summarization and prevents token bloat from the first tool call.

5

TERMINAL FOR APIS. Anything that can be done via an API (SAM.gov, USASpending, site crawl, revenue data) goes to Terminal. Chrome is for sites that require navigation and interpretation.

6

NO FULL PAGE REPRODUCTIONS. EVER. Snippets and summaries only.

FOUR SESSION TEMPLATES

Session 1 — CEO/Founder  ·  45 min
  • Walk me through a week in your life — where does most of your time actually go?
  • What's the one thing that, if fixed, would have the biggest impact?
  • Where do you see the company in 3 years and what would have to be true to get there?
  • What's been tried before and what happened?
  • Walk me through the journey of a single order from inquiry to shipped.
Session 2 — VP Sales  ·  45 min
  • Walk me through your sales process end to end — every step, every tool.
  • How do you track where each deal is right now?
  • What do you have to do manually that you wish happened automatically?
  • How do you follow up with leads from trade shows? What's the process?
  • If you could see one dashboard every morning, what would it show?
Session 3 — Operations  ·  45 min
  • How does manufacturing find out about a new order?
  • How do you know when you're running low on a part?
  • Walk me through your worst week operationally — what fell apart?
  • What software do you actually open every day?
  • What process eats the most time that a system should handle?
Session 4 — CFO/Finance  ·  30 min
  • What financial data can you not get today that you need?
  • How long does it take to close the books each month and why?
  • What's your biggest inventory-related financial exposure?
  • If you had a live dashboard showing one financial metric, what would it be?
  • What would you need to see to feel confident in a Phase 1 investment?
