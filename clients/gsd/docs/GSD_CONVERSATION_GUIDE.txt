================================================================================
DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Ask in this order. Do not skip ahead. Each question feeds the next.

--- Q1: SCHEDULE REALITY ---
"Walk me through a typical week for you — are you hitting full capacity
 on your procedure days, or are there gaps?"

   LISTEN FOR: no-show patterns, last-minute cancellations, empty afternoon
   slots. This surfaces Leak A without you naming it.

--- Q2: THE RECALL BACKLOG ---
"How are you handling patients who haven't been in for 18 months or more?
 Is your team working that list, or does it mostly sit?"

   LISTEN FOR: "we try but it's manual," "we use [X software]," "we don't
   really have a system." Any answer here except "it's fully automated"
   is a revenue leakage signal.

--- Q3: THE BOOKING MOMENT ---
"When someone calls after hours or fills out a form on your site — what
 happens? Does it go into a queue, or is there something that handles it
 immediately?"

   LISTEN FOR: "they call back the next morning," "we use a service,"
   "it goes to voicemail." After-hours capture failure is your Leak C.

--- Q4: THE REVIEW SITUATION ---
"How do you feel about where your online reputation is right now?
 I was looking at your Birdeye profile — you've got solid ratings, but
 what does the volume look like compared to where you want to be?"

   LISTEN FOR: "I know we should be doing more," "we send review requests
   sometimes," "I'm not sure who manages that." Birdeye at 5.0 with 176
   reviews is good but not dominant for a practice with his implant volume.

--- Q5: THE AI QUESTION ---
"Has anyone pitched you an AI solution in the last few months? I'm curious
 what you've seen — a lot of it is noise, and I want to know what's actually
 crossed your desk."

   LISTEN FOR: "we've looked at a few things," "nothing that made sense,"
   "our consultant handles that." This tells you whether you have a clear
   field or are competing with someone else's proposal.

THE PIVOT LINE
================================================================================

After Discovery Q3 or Q4 — when you've confirmed at least two leakage signals:

"Here's what I'm seeing across practices like yours. The problem isn't
 the tech — it's the gap between what these tools can do and how they're
 actually being implemented. Most practices either buy software they don't
 use, or they hire someone who knows the software but doesn't understand
 the practice. We sit in the middle. We figure out exactly where the money
 is leaking, we build the fix, and we run it. You keep doing dentistry."

(Pause. Let it land. Do not add to this immediately.)

================================================================================
THE ASK
================================================================================

Do not ask for a sale. Ask for a session.

"I'd like to do a 45-minute working session — I'll show you exactly where
 the numbers point and what a 90-day engagement would look like for your
 practice specifically. No deck, no pitch. Just the math and the plan.
 Does Thursday work, or is there a better day for you?"

   WHY THIS WORKS:
   - "Working session" implies you're already working, not pitching
   - "Specifically" signals this isn't a templated proposal
   - "No deck" lowers resistance from someone who's seen too many pitches
   - Offering a specific day forces a yes/no/counter, not a defer

OBJECTION HANDLERS
================================================================================

--- OBJECTION 1: "We're not really looking to add any new vendors right now." ---

"Totally understand — and to be clear, we're not a vendor. Vendors sell you
 something and move on. We're structured as a fractional ops partner.
 We're on the hook for outcomes, not just deliverables. The engagement only
 makes sense if the numbers work. Let me show you the math first and you
 can decide if it's worth a closer look."

--- OBJECTION 2: "I already have a marketing company / someone handling this." ---

"That's good — and this might complement what they're doing rather than
 replace it. What I'm focused on is the operational layer: scheduling
 efficiency, recall automation, after-hours capture. Most marketing companies
 aren't touching that. The question is whether the revenue you're leaving
 on the table is in the marketing layer or the ops layer. Usually it's ops."

--- OBJECTION 3: "What does something like this cost?" ---

"It depends on scope, but to give you a ballpark — engagements like this
 typically run $2,500–$4,500 a month, structured around a 90-day window.
 What I can tell you is the practices we've worked with typically recover
 more than that in the first 60 days just from reducing no-shows and
 re-activating dormant patients. I'd rather show you the math than quote
 you a number cold. That's what the working session is for."
