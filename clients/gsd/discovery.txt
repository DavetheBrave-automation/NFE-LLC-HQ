Q1 — REMINDER SYSTEM

QUESTION:
"What does your reminder and follow-up process look like right now —
is that something your team handles manually or do you have a system running it?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Biggest unknown in the entire research file. No texting platform was detected
anywhere in the tech stack scan — no Birdeye, Weave, NexHealth, RevenueWell,
Podium, Lighthouse 360, Solutionreach, or Demandforce found in page source.
Need to confirm whether (a) a vendor exists that we simply couldn't detect,
or (b) it's genuinely manual. This is the primary replacement or enhancement
opportunity depending on the answer.

IF A — Has a vendor:
→ Get the name. Ask what it does and doesn't do. Ask if the contract is annual.
→ Position as: "There's a newer generation of this that does X, Y, Z.
   Let's figure out if what you have is doing all of that."
→ Enhancement play — supplement don't replace, lower friction.

IF B — Manual only:
→ Zero switching friction. No contract to break. No vendor loyalty.
→ Automated reminders is Deliverable 1 in Phase 1. Immediate close.
→ "This is the fastest win we can build. It's running in two weeks."

Q2 — NO-SHOW PAIN

QUESTION:
"How big of an issue are no-shows for you — and with your condensed schedule,
what actually happens when someone cancels last minute?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Validates the $4,680/month leak estimate. The condensed 7am–3pm schedule means
unfilled slots almost never recover same-day — each no-show is a near-total
block loss. This is emotional pain, not just financial. Dr. Gardner feels this.
The question is how consciously.

IF A — High pain, vocal about it:
→ No-show prevention system leads the Phase 1 pitch.
→ "We can cut this in half in the first 30 days."
→ Automated 48hr / 24hr / 2hr reminder sequence + smart rescheduling.

IF B — "It's not that bad" or minimizes it:
→ "That's actually interesting — most practices running a condensed schedule
   feel this acutely. Let me show you what the data says about your specific
   appointment volume. It might be more than it feels like."
→ Run the math live: 8% no-show × 180 appts × $325 = $4,680/month.

Q3 — PATIENT INTAKE

QUESTION:
"When a new patient books — what does that process look like for them
and for your front desk before they walk in the door?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Confirms the paper PDF intake finding from the OSINT. Website has a downloadable
"NP-PAPERWORK" PDF — no Yapi, Modento, mConsent, or any digital intake platform
was detected. Quantifies front desk labor cost. Paper intake = 10–15 min of staff
processing per new patient before the appointment even starts.

IF A — All paper:
→ Digital intake is the fastest visible demo win. Buildable in Phase 1 week 1.
→ Show them what Yapi or mConsent looks like. "Patient fills this out on their
   phone before they arrive. Staff gets it formatted and ready. Zero paper."
→ Frame it as staff time recovered, not just tech upgrade.

IF B — Partially digital (some system exists):
→ "What platform is that? And what does it handle — just the forms,
   or does it connect to your scheduling software?"
→ Identify the gap, integrate rather than replace. Lower friction to engage.

Q4 — PRACTICE MANAGEMENT SOFTWARE

QUESTION:
"What's your main scheduling and charting software —
Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental, or something else?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
THE most critical technical unknown. All automation ROI depends on PMS API access.
Cannot scope Phase 1 without this answer. Everything — recall automation,
no-show prevention, digital intake, booking widget integration — runs through
the PMS. This is the foundation question.

IF A — Dentrix:
→ Well-documented API. Broad integration ecosystem.
→ NexHealth, Yapi, Weave all integrate cleanly.
→ Cleanest build path. Flag for David post-session.

IF B — Eaglesoft:
→ Patterson ecosystem. Slightly more complex but fully doable.
→ Integration layer adds a step but not a blocker.
→ Flag for research before final Phase 1 scope.

IF C — Open Dental:
→ Open source. Most flexible. Easiest to build custom integrations on.
→ Strong API. Community of integrations already exists.
→ Best technical case for custom NFE LLC automation.

IF D — Other (Curve, Denticon, cloud-based, etc.):
→ "I'll need to look into that before I can scope exactly how the
   automation connects. Give me a day — I'll come back with a specific plan."
→ Do not scope Phase 1 on the call. Research first.

Q5 — INACTIVE RECALL

QUESTION:
"Do you have a sense of how many patients from the last 2–3 years
have just gone quiet — and is there a process for reaching back out to them?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Validates the 144-patient inactive estimate and $3,180/month recovery calculation.
The key insight: these patients are already acquired. Zero new marketing spend to
reach them. They chose this practice once. Recall automation is the highest-ROI
automation in the practice — recover revenue from people who already trust the brand.

IF A — No recall process:
→ Reactivation campaign is the highest-ROI quick win.
→ Propose as a standalone deliverable even before the full retainer.
→ "We build a three-touch sequence over 30 days. Text, email, final call.
   You'll see patients booking in the first week."

IF B — Manual recall attempt:
→ "How often does that actually happen? What's the response rate?"
→ Manual recall at scale doesn't work — people don't respond to generic
   "we miss you" blasts from front desk staff.
→ "AI-personalized by last visit type and procedure creates 3–4× better
   response rates. Let me show you what that looks like."

Q6 — ONLINE BOOKING

QUESTION:
"Do patients ever ask about booking online, or does everything come through the phone?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Confirms the no-booking-widget gap. The OSINT found contact form only — no
real-time calendar, no third-party scheduler. The question approach matters:
if patients ask → pain is confirmed and Dr. Gardner is already aware of it.
If patients don't ask → "Silent exit is actually worse. They're leaving
without asking. You never know what you lost."

IF A — Patients ask for it:
→ Pain confirmed by the market, not just the research.
→ Online booking widget leads Phase 1. Easy close.
→ "This is the most immediate revenue unlock. Every visitor who can't book
   after hours is a lost appointment."

IF B — No complaints, everything comes through the phone:
→ "That's actually the problem — they're leaving without asking.
   Your website gets visitors who never convert because there's no
   booking option. They just leave. Silently."
→ Show the Leak C math: 400 visitors × 12% conversion × $750 = $36,000/mo opportunity.

Q8 — REVIEW MANAGEMENT

QUESTION:
"Your online reputation is genuinely exceptional — 176 reviews at a 5.0 on Birdeye.
Is that something you're actively managing or did it build organically?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Opens the automated review request conversation. Also reveals a critical detail:
Google shows 150 reviews. Birdeye shows 176. That 26-review gap means some
reviews are going to Birdeye instead of Google. Birdeye reviews don't move
Google local pack rankings. Google reviews do. The SEO gap may partly be a
platform-split issue, not just volume.

IF A — Organic, no active system:
→ "The risk is it stays that way until one bad week. And here's what most
   practices miss: Google reviews are what drive local pack ranking.
   Birdeye reviews don't move that needle. If 15% of your reviews are
   landing on Birdeye instead of Google, you're leaving SEO juice on the table."
→ Automated post-visit review request → Google specifically → fixes both.

IF B — Managed by a vendor or in-house:
→ "Who handles that? Is it bundled with your texting system?"
→ Get the vendor name. Cross-reference with Q1 answer.
→ If same vendor → potential for consolidated platform play.
→ If separate vendor → fragmentation argument.

Q9 — HIGH-VALUE CASE CONVERSION

QUESTION:
"For your implant and All-on-4 consultations — roughly what percentage
actually move forward with treatment, and what happens to the ones that don't?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
This is the highest-dollar gap in the entire practice. One lost All-on-4 case
= $20,000–$40,000. The OSINT found no post-visit follow-up or treatment plan
nurture sequence. An AI-powered follow-up sequence for unconverted implant/All-on-4
consults is the single highest-ROI automation in the entire engagement.
Even a 10% lift in conversion on 2 cases/month = $4K–$8K recovered.

IF A — Low conversion, no follow-up process:
→ Treatment plan nurture sequence becomes the flagship Phase 1 deliverable.
→ "This is where the money actually is. Not the booking widget, not the recall.
   One recovered All-on-4 case pays for the entire first year of NFE LLC."
→ This closes the engagement faster than any other finding.

IF B — High conversion, already handling it well:
→ "Even protecting that pipeline is worth systemizing. One unconverted case
   is $20,000–$40,000 walking out. If you do 10 consults a month and lose
   even one that was recoverable — that's the ROI on this entire engagement."
→ Frame as defensive automation, not gap-filling.

QUESTION:
"You posted in January about learning to use AI for dental images —
how far are you thinking about AI for the practice overall?
Is this something you're actively looking to move on or still in the exploring phase?"

WHY WE'RE ASKING:
Calibrates readiness to buy. The January post signals genuine curiosity — he's
thinking about this independently, which means David doesn't have to sell the
concept of AI. Only the application. This question separates "interested"
from "ready to move." It determines whether you close on the call or on the follow-up.

IF A — Ready to move, actively looking:
→ "Perfect. I want to show you three specific systems I'd build for you in the
   first 90 days that would pay for themselves in the first 30.
   Can we get 45 minutes on the calendar this week?"
→ This is the close. Lock the discovery session before you hang up.

IF B — Still exploring, not ready:
→ "Then the smartest first step is letting the data tell you where to focus.
   That's exactly what the FootprintIQ Brief does — it takes the guesswork
   out. Instead of trying to figure out what AI could do for your practice,
   you get a specific map of where the money is and what to build first."
→ Soft close to the $1,500 brief. Safer first step for a cautious buyer.
