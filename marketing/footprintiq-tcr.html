01

True Legal Identity

"Timeshare Contract Resolution" is not the legal name of the company consumers are contracting with. It is a trade name — a DBA — operated by Light On Marketing, LLC. That LLC is one of several entities owned and controlled by Rich Folk and William "Bo" Wilson, operating through a trust vehicle called the Collaborative Administrative Trust (CAT).

02

The "Founded 2009" Claim — Domain Evidence

TCR states repeatedly across their website and in BBB responses that they have been in business for 16 years, implying a founding date of approximately 2009–2010. WHOIS and Wayback Machine records directly contradict this claim.

▸ Domain Intelligence Finding

The domain timesharecontractresolution.com was registered on July 3, 2019 via GoDaddy.com, LLC. A Wayback Machine crawl of all captures between January 2009 and January 2012 returned zero results — the site did not exist in any form during the period they claim to have been operating.

Federal Litigation Record

The network of entities behind "Timeshare Contract Resolution" is the subject of significant ongoing federal litigation. The following cases are sourced from PACER via CourtListener's RECAP archive.

⬛ Active Federal Case — M.D. Florida
Wyndham Vacation Ownership v. Pandora Marketing / Folk / Wilson et al. 6:19-cv-01908

Filed October 2019. Still active as of April 9, 2026. Wyndham sued the Pandora/Folk/Wilson network for trademark infringement and unlawful business practices. The TCR website appears as a court exhibit in a 2023 motion citing continued unlawful operation. Magistrate Judge recommended a $16,231,197.88 disgorgement award plus a permanent injunction against the core network. Pandora filed a retaliatory countersuit (6:23-cv-1513) which is also pending.

⬛ Resolved Federal Case — C.D. California
Wyndham Vacation Ownership v. Miranda Dempsey (MXM Legal) 8:24-cv-01747

Filed August 2024. The complaint explicitly names Light On Marketing, LLC d/b/a Timeshare Contract Resolution as part of an unlawful attorney referral scheme. The defendant attorney — who was accepting client referrals from Folk/Wilson's network — consented to a permanent injunction. Case dismissed November 20, 2024.

Consumer Complaint Patterns

A review of 12 published BBB complaints, Trustpilot reviews, and independent consumer accounts reveals five consistent failure patterns. The following are direct consumer accounts from BBB filings.

  • 01
    Communication blackout after payment. The single most consistent theme: once money changes hands, TCR becomes nearly unreachable. Clients report months of unanswered emails, staff who cannot locate their case files, and complete turnover of assigned contacts.
  • 02
    Verbal promises the written contract does not support. Clients were told TCR would "go to court if necessary," "get their money back," and "fix their credit." TCR's BBB responses repeatedly clarify: "We never promise litigation." The gap between the sales pitch and the contract is a primary driver of complaints.
  • 03
    Out-of-jurisdiction or unqualified attorneys. Multiple complainants discovered their assigned attorney was not licensed in the state where their timeshare was located. In one case, this resulted in a court judgment being entered against the client.
  • 04
    Hidden stacked fees. After paying an initial deposit — sometimes via third-party financing — clients are informed they must pay a separate attorney retainer ($950 in at least two documented cases) before any work begins.
  • 05
    Default strategy not disclosed upfront. Consumer reviews describe the realization that TCR's exit method is advising clients to stop paying their timeshare, let it default, and wait for the company to charge off the debt — something any owner can do independently, at no cost.

Our Finding

Timeshare Contract Resolution presents itself as a 16-year-old consumer advocacy leader. The documentary record tells a different story. The brand is a trade name for Light On Marketing, LLC — one of several entities controlled by Rich Folk and Bo Wilson, whose primary enterprise (Pandora Marketing / Timeshare Compliance) has declared bankruptcy twice and faces a recommended federal disgorgement award of over $16 million.

The domain that carries the "Timeshare Contract Resolution" brand was registered in July 2019 — a decade after their claimed founding date, with zero archived web presence to bridge the gap. Federal courts have already entered a permanent injunction against an attorney who accepted client referrals from this network.

Consumers considering this company are advised to: (1) contact their timeshare developer directly about any official exit or deed-back program before paying any third party; (2) demand all fees in writing before any screening call or document submission; (3) independently verify that any assigned attorney is licensed in the relevant jurisdiction; and (4) review the FTC's consumer guidance on timeshare exit companies at consumer.ftc.gov.
